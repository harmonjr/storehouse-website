## Proposed Changes

### Change 1: Strengthen biblical attribution in stewardship framing

**Why:** Voice Alignment -- CIS updated to "Attributive, not vague" and "Biblically grounded, not preachy" with instruction to be direct: "The Bible teaches..." and credit God as the source. The article has one direct reference (line 79) but could be slightly more attributive without becoming preachy.

> **Current (line 79):**
> That weekly confirmation is what turns biblical stewardship -- the conviction that what you have is God's, and your job is to manage it faithfully -- from something you believe in to something you do.

> **Proposed:**
> That weekly confirmation is what turns stewardship from something you believe in to something you do. The Bible describes a posture -- that what you have is God's, and your job is to manage it faithfully. Ten minutes a week is where that posture becomes practice.

This change separates the parenthetical into its own sentence, making the biblical attribution more direct rather than subordinate.

---

### Change 2: Remove near-duplicate line

**Why:** Craft Quality -- Prose Rhythm. Lines 31 and 67 make essentially the same point in nearly the same words.

> **Current (line 31):**
> The act of showing up -- downloading, importing, looking -- is what turns data into understanding. Ten minutes of attention does what twelve months of auto-synced notifications can't.

> **Current (line 67):**
> Ten minutes of intentional attention to your finances does more than twelve months of auto-synced data you never really look at.

> **Proposed:** Keep line 67 (it is the stronger version -- "you never really look at" adds the human truth). Revise line 31 to say something different:

> The act of showing up -- downloading, importing, looking -- is what turns data into understanding. You can't steward what you haven't seen.

This eliminates the repetition and connects to the CIS founding truth "You can't steward what you can't see."

---

### Change 3: Tighten "Why Weekly and Not Daily" section opening

**Why:** AI Tells -- Hedge Phrase. "Some financial advice tells you to check your money daily" is vague. Who says this? It reads like filler setup.

> **Current (line 49):**
> Some financial advice tells you to check your money daily. We tried that. It was exhausting.

> **Proposed:**
> We tried checking daily. It was exhausting.

The point lands faster. The reader does not need the setup of "some financial advice."

---

### Change 4: Section header could be more specific

**Why:** Craft Quality -- Engagement. "What Actually Happens During the Review" (line 69) is functional but flat. It could create more curiosity.

> **Current (line 69):**
> ## What Actually Happens During the Review

> **Proposed:**
> ## Where the Hierarchy Stops Being Theory

This creates pull -- the reader wants to know how a principle becomes real.

## Craft Quality

- **Near-duplicate phrasing:** Lines 31 and 67 make the same point twice (see Change 2).

- **Mild symmetrical structure:** The four steps in "The Ritual" section (lines 37-45) each follow a "Step N: [bold]. [Explanation]" pattern. This is appropriate for a how-to list and does not read as AI-generated -- it reads as instructional. No change needed.

Sentence length varies well throughout. "It's not dramatic. It's not stressful. It's ten minutes." (line 15) followed by the longer line 17 is textbook rhythm. "You checked. You know. You're good."
