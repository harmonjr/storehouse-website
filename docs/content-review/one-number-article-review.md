## Proposed Changes

### Change 1: Reduce "47 categories" repetition across the corpus

**Priority:** Medium
**Why:** Voice alignment -- the phrase appears 3 times in this article and 4 times in Article #3. It is stronger in Article #3 where it is the structural metaphor. In this article, the third usage (line 81) can be varied without losing impact.

> **Current (line 81):**
> Here's the real insight: traditional budgets fail because they require constant discipline in 47 different areas. Every purchase is a decision. Every category is a potential failure.

> **Proposed:**
> Here's the real insight: traditional budgets fail because they distribute discipline across every single purchase. Every trip to the store is a decision. Every category is a potential failure.

This preserves the rhythm and point while freeing "47 categories" to belong more distinctly to Article #3.

---

### Change 2: Tighten the "discipline shift" section to avoid restating what was already said

**Priority:** Medium
**Why:** Factual consistency (internal) -- Lines 83-85 restate the formula steps almost verbatim from lines 28-29. In a 1,050-word piece, this overlap is noticeable and slightly dilutes the section's punchline (line 87, "That's wise stewardship").

> **Current (lines 83-85):**
> How much do you want to give? How much do you want to save? What are your fixed bills? Make those three decisions, and every spending decision after that becomes simple. The hard work is already done. The priorities are already handled. What remains is yours.

> **Proposed:**
> Decide what you give, what you save, and what your bills require. Three decisions. After that, every spending choice is the same question: is my number above zero? The hard work is already done. What remains is yours.

This version is tighter, avoids restating the formula, and connects back to the article's most memorable line ("is my number above zero?") to create a structural echo.

---

### Change 3: Strengthen the cross-reference to Article #1

**Priority:** Low
**Why:** Cross-content consistency -- Line 29 references "the hierarchy we talked about in 'Give First'" but Article #1's title is "Give First, Save Second, Spend What Remains." Using the full title (or close to it) strengthens the link for readers who may encounter the articles independently.

> **Current (line 29):**
> Notice the order. It follows the hierarchy we talked about in "Give First" -- giving comes out first, saving comes second, bills come third.

> **Proposed:**
> Notice the order. It follows the hierarchy from "Give First, Save Second, Spend What Remains" -- giving comes out first, saving comes second, bills come third.

This is a minor polish. Either version works, but the full title reinforces the companion article's core phrase.
