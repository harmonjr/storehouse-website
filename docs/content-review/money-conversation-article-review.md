## Proposed Changes

### Change 1: Soften the "number one source of conflict" claim

**Why:** Factual consistency. The claim is directionally accurate but stated as absolute fact. Some research supports it; other studies rank communication or household labor higher. Stating it as widely observed rather than definitive protects credibility.

> **Current (line 17):**
> Money is the number one source of conflict in marriages -- not because couples are bad with money, but because they're working with different numbers in their heads.

> **Proposed:**
> Money is one of the most common sources of conflict in marriages -- not because couples are bad with money, but because they're working with different numbers in their heads.

**Priority:** Medium. The current version is not wrong, but "number one" is a specific claim that invites "actually, research shows..." pushback. "One of the most common" is equally strong rhetorically and more defensible.

---

### Change 2: Differentiate the "$40 dinner" line from Article #2

**Why:** Cross-content consistency. The sentence "the fight was never about the $40 dinner. It was about not having a shared definition of 'affordable'" appears identically in Article #2 (line 65) and this article (line 21). The article acknowledges the overlap ("We introduced this idea in 'The One Number That Tells You the Truth'"), but the verbatim repetition is noticeable. Since this article expands the idea, it should restate it in its own language.

> **Current (lines 21-22):**
> Here's what most couples don't realize: the fight was never about the $40 dinner. It was about not having a shared definition of "affordable."

> **Proposed:**
> Here's what most couples don't realize: the fight was never about the restaurant bill. It was about two people answering "can we afford this?" with different math in their heads.

**Priority:** Medium. The "$40 dinner" is a strong specific detail, but using the identical phrasing across two articles makes both feel templated. The proposed version keeps the core insight while giving this article its own language. It also sets up the "different math" framing that the next paragraph (lines 25-27) develops.

---

### Change 3: Ground the "couples tell us" claim

**Why:** Factual accuracy / Financial Accuracy standards. Line 59 states "Couples tell us they stop dreading the money conversation." If this is based on real user feedback, it should stay -- it is powerful social proof. If it is aspirational, it should be reframed to avoid implying a user base that may not yet exist or testimonials that haven't been collected.

> **Current (line 59):**
> Couples tell us they stop dreading the money conversation. It becomes what it should have been all along -- two people who share a life making decisions together with the same information.

> **Proposed (if based on real feedback -- keep as is):**
> Couples tell us they stop dreading the money conversation. It becomes what it should have been all along -- two people who share a life making decisions together with the same information.

> **Proposed (if not yet based on real feedback):**
> The money conversation stops being something to dread. It becomes what it should have been all along -- two people who share a life making decisions together with the same information.

**Priority:** Medium. This is a judgment call that depends on whether the claim is grounded. If real feedback exists, the current version is stronger. If not, the alternative avoids implying social proof that does not exist yet, which the Financial Accuracy section of the Content Integrity System specifically guards against (line 196: "Never let marketing outpace reality").

---

### Change 4: Tighten the cross-reference phrasing at line 67

**Why:** Voice alignment. "The weekly ritual from 'The Weekly Ritual'" is tautological and reads awkwardly. The cross-reference is valuable, but the phrasing can be cleaner.

> **Current (line 67):**
> The weekly ritual from "The Weekly Ritual" becomes a couples practice.

> **Proposed:**

**Priority:** Low. If the article titles are hyperlinked on the website, keeping the title reference has navigation value. But "the weekly ritual from 'The Weekly Ritual'" is a sentence that calls attention to itself for the wrong reasons. The proposed version trusts the reader to make the connection, or the site can add the hyperlink on "weekly import ritual."

## Overall Assessment

"The Money Conversation" is a strong Tier 3 article that earns its place in the Foundations series. The opening scene is the best in the collection -- visceral, specific, and immediately recognizable to any couple who has had the silent drive home. The "permission trap" section (lines 29-37) is the article's standout contribution: it names the spender/police dynamic with empathy and precision, giving couples language for a pattern they experience but rarely articulate. None are structural. The single most important change is Change 2 (differentiating the "$40 dinner" line), because verbatim repetition across two articles undermines the sense that each piece was individually crafted. This article is ready to publish with or without the proposed revisions.
